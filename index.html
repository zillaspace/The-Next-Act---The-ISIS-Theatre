Campaign Positioning

Preserve Aspen's Cultural Heart

The ISIS Theatre should be positioned as a cultural landmark, not only as a building with naming opportunities. For affluent donors, the emotional purchase is legacy. The financial gift becomes a permanent place in the Aspen story.

The more powerful message is not help us renovate. It is help write the next act. Naming rights turn patrons into producers of Aspen's future.

The Next Act

A campaign with the glamour of a premiere, the seriousness of preservation, and the intimacy of a private Aspen salon.

Donor Recognition

The Aspen Story Wall

Do not build a flat donor wall. Build an elegant story installation.

Each major donor can have a refined bronze or stone plaque with a QR story page, a personal Aspen memory, a portrait, a quote, and connection to film, culture, or community. It becomes part museum, part private club, part civic archive.

This gives donors something far more valuable than their name in a list. It gives them context.

Presentation Strategy

How To Sell It

One

Lead With Legacy

Start every conversation with preserving a living piece of Aspen history, not with square footage.

Two

Match Donor Identity To Space

A filmmaker belongs in a screening room. A writer belongs in the café. A family foundation belongs in the atrium. A social host belongs at the bar.

Three

Create Scarcity

Use purchased opportunities and limited founder slots to create momentum. Scarcity is essential in a market like Aspen.

Four

Make Recognition Beautiful

Use bronze, stone, leather, glass, archival photography, and warm light. Recognition should feel like a private club, not a municipal plaque board.
